Project Intro

Our project looks at how population & GDP affect how well countries perform at the Olympics. We examined all Olympic Games, from 1960 to today.



Things to note:

  • Country names and boundries have changed in the last sixty years. As an example: if you are looking for the full Olympic of a country that was renamed like Russia, you will need to look for both the Soviet Union and Russia.
  • Not all countries have competed in both the summer and winter olympics, and thus you may seem some "blank" graphs due to lack of participation.
  • The most recent Summer Olympics had 306 events, with a total of 918 medals. In comparison, the most recent Winter Olympics had only 102 events, the first Winter Olympics to surpass 100 events. Keep this in mind when comparing Summer numbers to Winter numbers.
  • Winter and Summer Olympics were held in the same year until 1992, until they split to be on separate four year cycles. The next Winter Olympics was held in 1994.
